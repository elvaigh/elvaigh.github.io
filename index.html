PhD : Incremental content to data linking leveraging ontological knowledge in data journalism 2018-2020
My thesis is part of the iCODA project. Taking data journalism as an emblematic usecase, the goal of iCODA is to develop the scientific and technological foundations for knowledgemediated user-in-the-loop collaborative data analytics on heterogeneous information sources. My PhD stands at the crossroad of content analysis, in particular natural language processing (NLP), and semantic knowledge bases, notably knowledge representation and reasoning and semantic web (SW). The overall goal consists in formalizing and experimenting approaches that permit constant back and forth between the two worlds. We will especially consider how knowledge bases can be used to improve content interpretation and linking, or to establish relations between statements in content and public data resources. Taking data journalism as an application scenario, we will seek to develop knowledge-aware content interpretation approaches to three main problems: (i) information extraction and entity linking; (ii) linking statements to external data sources, e.g., for fact checking; (iii) semantic interpretation of links, i.e., being able to explain why two pieces of content are related or how a statement relates to some particular data.
